The question

What we set out to answer

This is a TIME study, not a performance study. We're answering "how do sellers spend their time" — not "who's winning". That distinction shapes what we measured and deliberately left out.

Core question
"In a typical week, where does a seller's time actually go — across meetings, calls, and digital channels?"
Answered through three sub-questions, each addressed in a tab.

Within customer-facing time, what's the mix? And digitally — where does Slack time land?

Sub-question 3 → Customers + Cuts tabs
Where does customer time land, and how does it vary by role and region?

1 · Metrics & classifications

Time metrics

Calendar h/wk
Sum of Google Calendar event durations in Q1 ÷ 12.86 working weeks.
Customer-facing h/wk
Subset of Calendar where the meeting title matches customer-facing patterns. 10 sub-categories — see table below.
Internal h/wk
Calendar minus customer-facing. Team syncs, exec reviews, 1:1s.

40 − Calendar − Gong − Slack-active. Deep work, email, breaks, time off.

Channels the rep posted in during Q1.

How the classifier actually runs (full logic)

An event lands in a sub-category when all three are true:

  1. It's already classified as customer-facing at the top level (top == "customer_direct") — meaning the title or attendees match customer-facing patterns at the parent level.
  2. The patterns above are checked in order (QBR → onboarding → renewal → demo/pitch → strategy → support → exec_review → event_dinner). First match wins.
  3. If none match, the catch-all rule applies: events with a Google Calendar recurringEventId become Recurring Sync / 1:1; everything else becomes Other / Ad-hoc.

Why Recurring is 51%: most customer-facing recurring titles are generic ("Customer X weekly sync", "Adi / Marvin weekly 1:1") and don't include category keywords. They fall through every specific pattern and land in this catch-all. The actual content of these meetings (deal review? troubleshooting? renewal?) isn't visible from the title alone.

Source: scripts/sub_categorize.py — 10 named patterns + 2 fallback rules. Can be extended (e.g., split Recurring by daily/weekly/monthly frequency) without touching upstream data.

How the channel classifier actually runs (full logic)

Each channel is bucketed by name pattern. Patterns checked in order — first match wins:

  1. customer_account: starts with ads-, ads_, ext-, ext_, external-, external_, moloco-, moloco_, OR contains -moloco-, _moloco_, out_moloco, share_moloco
  2. customer_vip: contains vip or -customer-
  3. internal_tools: contains speedboat, searchlight, opsportal, bizdash, bq-agent, salesforce_, salesforce-, looker
  4. customer_account (integration projects): contains integration-, phase1, phase2, tencent, tubi, kalshi, nexon, mistplay
  5. adops_internal: starts with adops-, adops_, gss-adops
  6. product_eng: starts with dsp-, mcm-, msm-, next-, ml- OR ends with -eng
  7. cross_functional: contains gtm-, xfn-, project-, stratops, speedboat, ai-builders, bizops, salesops, post-it
  8. sales_region: starts with amer-, apac-, emea-, chn-, ctv-sales- OR contains pipeline
  9. social: contains random, recognition, talent, ai-hub, nho-, mba-, hack-
  10. admin_announce: contains general, all-hands, molo-co-updates, important-, it-, people-
  11. dm: Slack IM or MPIM type (DMs and group DMs)
  12. other: fallback for anything that didn't match

What this tells us — and what it doesn't

Slack channels are an engagement proxy — not a time-in-tool measure. 453 Speedboat-related messages from 18 reps tells us reps discuss Speedboat in Slack and ask questions about it. It does NOT tell us how many minutes/week they spent IN Speedboat clicking through reports. To answer that, we need tool-side telemetry.

Three honest caveats on these estimates:
  1. The Slack proxy only captures reps who DISCUSS a tool. A rep can use Speedboat without ever mentioning it in Slack — they're invisible to our engaged-reps count, so the inference under-counts adoption.
  2. Sessions/wk and min/session are anchored to gut-feel-plus-industry-norms, not measurement. Real numbers could easily be ±50%.
  3. Workspace estimates assume ALL 124 reps use Gmail/Drive/etc. roughly equally — likely true for Gmail, less true for Sheets/Slides where adoption varies.
Direct telemetry (Workspace Admin SDK, internal BQ logs) collapses all three caveats. The tables above are a placeholder to make the residual interpretable until we have that.
FAQ

Questions you'll probably have

Pre-empting the common pushback. Click any question to expand.

Why only 145 reps when there are more in sales?
Sales-org cohort (Leadership / Director / Sales·GM·AE / Agency / NBS·SDR / CS·CSM) AND ≥15 Q1 calendar events. The threshold filters mid-quarter joiners, reps on leave, or non-customer-facing roles. PSO, GDS, marketing intentionally excluded.
Why this exact time window?
Q1 2026 (Jan 1 – Mar 31) = 12.86 working weeks — a full quarter. Q1 2027 = first true year-over-year.
How is a meeting classified as 'customer-facing'?
By title regex. QBR, demo, pitch, intro, sync-with-customer-domain, renewal, kickoff. Internal-only meetings excluded. ~15-20% of events land in 'Other / Ad-hoc' due to ambiguous titles.
Why are Slack messages capped at 78/wk?
Slack's search.messages API returns at most 1,000 results per query. ÷ 12.86 weeks ≈ 78/wk. Very-most-active reps are clipped. Cohort medians unaffected (only top decile).
Why doesn't this include closed-won outcomes?
This is a TIME study, not a performance study. Outcomes analysis exists separately; conclusions there are correlational not causal.
Why medians instead of means?
Calendar hours + Slack volume have right-skewed distributions. Median = the typical rep. P25/P75/P90 shown so you see the shape.
Are the medians comparable across roles?
Yes for the cuts table. Each role's median is within its own cohort — fair role-to-role comparison.
Why is CHN lower than other regions?
CHN reps use Lark + WeChat for most internal + customer messaging. Slack metrics under-measure their actual digital presence.
How do we know the channel classification is correct?
Rule-based on channel name patterns. Spot-checked highest-volume channels; ~95% match intuition. Unmatched land in 'Other'.
Can I drill into a specific rep?
Not in this fact pack by design — cohort-level patterns only. Persona profiles in the Quartiles tab show 4 representative reps with real Q1 data.
How often is this refreshed?
Quarterly. Same data sources, same scripts. Next refresh = early July (Q2 2026).
What's the right way to use this?
(1) Baseline what 'typical' looks like per role/region. (2) Spot outliers (P90/max column). (3) Compare quarter-over-quarter. Don't use for individual performance reviews — too many confounders.
